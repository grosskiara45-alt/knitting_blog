DROPS Design "Summer Vines Vest": Current WIP

Date: March 30th 2026

As we enter the wonderful season of spring, I wanted to challenge myself with creating a vest that I can wear in both cooler and warmer weather! As I researched patterns, I found DROPS Design ‘Summer Vines Vest’ on Ravelry. To create this pattern, I am using acrylic yarn as that is what I have on hand (my goal for this year is to knit through all of my acrylic yarn before investing in high quality since I made the mistake of bulk buying acrylic yarn first - yikes!)

This design incorporates many knitting techniques that I have done in the past as well as new techniques that I am excited to be learning. Right now, I am working through the back portion of the vest, with only a couple more inches to go before I cast off the neck area. Here is a fun little time lapse of my current progress!

As of right now, here are my key takeaways:

  1. Finally figuring out the filter system on Ravelry: In the past, a lot of the patterns I have made came from YouTube tutorials so when it comes to Ravelry, I am still getting used to the sites’ navigation (which has been fun to explore as someone currently learning web development). Because of this, I have finally figured out how to not only filter patterns by craft, needle size, etc. but also by straight needles vs in the round.
  2. Knitting is more fun when there are new challenges: While I love a standard stockinette stitch in the round, this pattern has made me think ahead more as I’m knitting as well as reflect on techniques that I’ve used in the past versus the new ways that I’m using them in this pattern. For example, when increasing by one stitch in the past, I would only go through the loop. However, this pattern incorporates the Yarn Over method which has been fun to not only do but see how this method of adding stitches creates an interesting look.
  3. Using the Notes App to keep track of rows and pattern steps: Since this is my first time reading a pattern chart, I knew going in that I would need a way to keep track of which row I am on as well as the steps for each row. In the past, I have merely used pen and paper to keep count of the rows. However, now I am using the notes app to not only keep track of the row count but also break down the Pattern 1 steps as well! This has been the most helpful in the process so far!

I am super excited to continue creating this pattern especially as I get close to finishing the back section. My goal is to finish this piece by May 1st and share it with you guys. Thanks for checking out this post and I’ll see you in the next one!
