Kiara Gross: Beginner Knitter and Frontend Web Developer

Hi everyone! My name is Kiara Gross, and I am jumping back into the world of web development and knitting! I created this website to track my knitting progress and hone in on my newly learned web development skills.


My journey started back in 2020. At the time, I was attending the University of Cincinnati, studying Media Production and taking a required HTML course. I have always had an interest in visual composition, but that class introduced me to web development with Adobe Dreamweaver. Being in college during COVID came with its own struggles, and my mental health was not the best. To cope with the trying times, I decided to pick up knitting. Some of my first projects included cute tank tops from YouTube tutorials. Knitting became a fun hobby for me. However, after a while, I stopped knitting and did other hobbies. I also started to use paid website builders like Wix and Squarespace as a shortcut for making websites. So, I stopped learning knitting and web development.


I got back into both in 2025, right after leaving the film industry. While I had learned a lot as a filmmaker and studio manager at my first full-time job, I realized how much more I loved visual layouts and designs. I also realized how much that job was draining me! Towards the end of my time at that job, I discovered Code: You and applied. Then, I got into the program! I am a current Code: You Web Development student, and we have to create a small project. As I was knitting my first sweater, it hit me that knitting and web development actually have a lot in common. From reading patterns to code, or making applications to sweaters, both are logic-based and have their own languages.


I will be using this website not only as a sandbox for my Code: You coursework but also as an outlet for my knitting journey! Thanks for reading, and I look forward to sharing my stories with you!
